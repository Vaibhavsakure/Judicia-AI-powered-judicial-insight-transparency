THE INDIAN PENAL CODE (IPC) - COMPREHENSIVE DATABASE
====================================================================

=== CHAPTER I: INTRODUCTION ===

Section 1: Title and Extent of Operation
The Code extends to the whole of India. It applies to every person within India, regardless of nationality, and to Indian citizens outside India for certain offences.

Section 2: Punishment of Offences Committed Within India
Every person shall be liable to punishment under this Code for every act or omission contrary to its provisions, of which he shall be guilty within India.

Section 3: Punishment of Offences Committed Beyond India
Any person liable, by any Indian law, to be tried for an offence committed beyond India shall be dealt with according to the provisions of this Code.

Section 4: Extension of Code to Extra-Territorial Offences
Provisions extend to offences committed by Indian citizens outside India, Indian ships, and aircraft registered in India.

Section 5: Certain Laws Not to be Affected by This Act
Nothing in this Act shall affect provisions of any special or local law, the Indian Army Act, Navy Act, or Air Force Act.

=== CHAPTER II: GENERAL EXPLANATIONS ===

Section 6: Definitions in the Code to be Understood Subject to Exceptions
Throughout this Code every definition is subject to exceptions expressed or understood.

Section 7: Sense of Expression Once Explained
Every expression explained in any part of this Code is used in every part in conformity with the explanation.

Section 8: Gender
The pronoun "he" and its derivatives include "she" when applicable.

Section 9: Number
Unless contrary appears, words in singular include plural and vice versa.

Section 10: "Man", "Woman"
"Man" denotes a male human being of any age; "Woman" denotes a female human being of any age.

Section 11: "Person"
"Person" includes any company, association, or body of persons, whether incorporated or not.

Section 12: "Public"
"Public" includes any class of the public or any community.

Section 13: "Queen"
Now read as "Government" post-independence.

Section 14: "Servant of Government"
Denotes any officer or servant continued, appointed, or employed by the Central or State Government.

Section 15: "Servant of Government" - Additional Definition
Includes judges, officers of courts, commissioners appointed by courts, officers of armed forces.

Section 16: "Government"
Now means the Central Government or State Government as applicable.

Section 17: "Government of India"
Repealed and replaced by adaptation orders post-independence.

Section 18: "India"
Now refers to the territory of India as defined in the Constitution.

Section 19: "Judge"
Denotes a person empowered by law to give a definitive judgment in legal proceedings.

Section 20: "Court of Justice"
A judge empowered by law to act judicially alone, or a body of judges required to act judicially together.

Section 21: "Public Servant"
Includes judges, government officers, officers of courts, police, army, navy, air force officers, village officers, etc. (18 categories listed).

Section 22: "Movable Property"
Property of every description except immovable property (land and buildings attached to earth).

Section 23: "Wrongful Gain"
Gaining property to which person is not legally entitled; wrongful loss is depriving person of property to which they are entitled.

Section 24: "Dishonestly"
Doing anything with intention to cause wrongful gain to one person or wrongful loss to another.

Section 25: "Fraudulently"
Doing anything with intent to defraud but not otherwise.

Section 26: "Reason to Believe"
Person has reason to believe a fact if he has sufficient cause to believe that fact but not otherwise.

Section 27: Property in Possession of Wife, Clerk, or Servant
When property is in possession of a person's wife, clerk, or servant on account of that person, it is in that person's possession.

Section 28: "Counterfeit"
A person counterfeits who causes one thing to resemble another with intention that the imitation be used as genuine.

Section 29: "Document"
Any matter expressed or described upon any substance by marks capable of being read or understood.

Section 29A: "Electronic Record"
Data generated, stored, received, or sent in electronic form which is capable of being retrieved or processed.

Section 30: "Valuable Security"
Document which is or purports to be a document whereby any legal right is created, extended, transferred, restricted, extinguished, or released.

Section 31: "A Will"
A testamentary document expressing intention of testator with respect to property which he desires to be carried into effect after his death.

Section 32: "Words Referring to Acts Include Illegal Omissions"
In every part of this Code, words referring to acts done also include illegal omissions.

Section 33: "Act", "Omission"
"Act" denotes a series of acts as well as single act; "omission" denotes a series of omissions as well as single omission.

Section 34: Acts Done by Several Persons in Furtherance of Common Intention
When criminal act is done by several persons in furtherance of common intention of all, each is liable as if done by him alone.

Section 35: When Such an Act is Criminal by Reason of Its Being Done with a Criminal Knowledge or Intention
Whenever an act, which is criminal only by reason of its being done with a criminal knowledge or intention, is done by several persons, each having that knowledge or intention, is liable.

Section 36: Effect Caused Partly by Act and Partly by Omission
Wherever the causing of a certain effect by an act or omission is an offence, it is the same offence if caused partly by act and partly by omission.

Section 37: Co-operation by Doing One of Several Acts Constituting an Offence
When an offence is committed by several acts, whoever cooperates by doing any one of those acts is guilty of that offence.

Section 38: Persons Concerned in Criminal Act May be Guilty of Different Offences
Where several persons are engaged in commission of a criminal act, they may be guilty of different offences by means of that act.

Section 39: "Voluntarily"
A person is said to cause an effect voluntarily when he causes it by means whereby he intended to cause it.

Section 40: "Offence"
A thing punishable under this Code or under any special or local law.

Section 41: "Special Law"
A law applicable to a particular subject.

Section 42: "Local Law"
A law applicable only to a particular part of India.

Section 43: "Illegal", "Legally Bound to Do"
"Illegal" is applicable to everything which is an offence or prohibited by law; "legally bound to do" means an act contrary to law.

Section 44: "Injury"
Any harm whatever illegally caused to person, body, mind, reputation, or property.

Section 45: "Life"
Denotes life of a human being, unless contrary appears from context.

Section 46: "Death"
Denotes death of a human being, unless contrary appears from context.

Section 47: "Animal"
Denotes any living creature other than a human being.

Section 48: "Vessel"
Denotes anything made for conveyance of human beings or property by water.

Section 49: "Year", "Month"
"Year" denotes a year reckoned according to British calendar; "Month" denotes a month reckoned according to British calendar.

Section 50: "Section"
Denotes a section of the Code.

Section 51: "Oath"
Includes a solemn affirmation substituted by law for an oath.

Section 52: "Good Faith"
Nothing is said to be done or believed in "good faith" unless done or believed without examination and inquiry which the circumstances require.

Section 52A: "Harbour"
Except in Section 157, includes supply of food, drink, clothing, shelter, or means of conveyance to offender.

=== CHAPTER III: PUNISHMENTS ===

Section 53: Punishments
The punishments under this Code are: Death, Imprisonment for life, Imprisonment (rigorous or simple), Forfeiture of property, Fine.

Section 54: Commutation of Sentence of Death
Appropriate Government may commute death sentence to: Imprisonment for life, Imprisonment (rigorous or simple) for up to 14 years, Fine.

Section 55: Commutation of Sentence of Imprisonment for Life
May be commuted to imprisonment (rigorous or simple) not exceeding 14 years.

Section 55A: Definition of "Appropriate Government"
For offences under armed forces acts: Central Government; otherwise: Government to which the offender is subject or where offence committed.

Section 56: Sentence of Europeans and Americans to Penal Servitude
Repealed.

Section 57: Fractions of Terms of Punishment
In calculating fractions of punishment, imprisonment for life equals 20 years.

Section 58: Offenders Sentenced to Transportation - How Dealt With Until Transported
Repealed.

Section 59: Transportation Instead of Imprisonment
Repealed.

Section 60: Sentence May be (in Certain Cases of Imprisonment) Wholly or Partly Rigorous or Simple
Court may award wholly rigorous, wholly simple, or partly rigorous and partly simple imprisonment.

Section 61: Sentence of Forfeiture of Property
Court may sentence offender to forfeiture of property in cases specified.

Section 62: Forfeiture of Property - What Included
In cases where forfeiture is awarded, property liable includes property possessed at time of apprehension and subsequently.

Section 63: Amount of Fine
Amount of fine is unlimited unless otherwise specified in the section.

Section 64: Sentence of Imprisonment for Non-Payment of Fine
Where fine only is ordered, imprisonment for non-payment not to exceed: 2 years if offence punishable with imprisonment for over 1 year; 6 months if punishable with imprisonment for not more than 1 year; 2 months if offence not punishable with imprisonment.

Section 65: Limit to Imprisonment for Non-Payment of Fine (Multiple Sentences)
Total imprisonment for non-payment of fines in multiple sentences not to exceed: 1/4 of maximum imprisonment term or 3 years (whichever less).

Section 66: Description of Imprisonment for Non-Payment of Fine
Imprisonment for non-payment of fine is simple.

Section 67: Imprisonment to Terminate on Payment of Fine
Imprisonment for non-payment terminates when fine is paid.

Section 68: Imprisonment in Default of Payment of Fine
When offence punishable with both imprisonment and fine: if fine not paid, court may order imprisonment in default.

Section 69: Termination of Imprisonment on Payment of Proportional Part of Fine
If total fine divisible, payment of proportional part releases prisoner proportionally.

Section 70: Fine Leviable Within Six Years or During Imprisonment - Death Not to Discharge Property from Liability
Fine may be levied on property within 6 years or during imprisonment; death does not discharge property from liability.

Section 71: Limit of Punishment of Offence Made Up of Several Offences
Where single act/series constitutes multiple offences, offender not liable for punishment exceeding severest punishment for any one offence.

Section 72: Punishment of Person Guilty of One of Several Offences (Judgment Uncertain Which)
In case of doubt, offender may be punished for the offence with lowest punishment.

Section 73: Solitary Confinement
Courts may direct rigorous imprisonment offenders be kept in solitary confinement for portions not exceeding: 1 month at a time (max 3 months in year).

Section 74: Limit of Solitary Confinement
Solitary confinement not to exceed 3 months if sentence is for 6 months or more; proportionally less for shorter sentences.

Section 75: Enhanced Punishment for Certain Offences After Previous Conviction
If person convicted of offences under certain sections and previously convicted 3+ times for similar offences, may be liable to imprisonment for life or 10 years with fine.

=== CHAPTER IV: GENERAL EXCEPTIONS ===

Section 76: Act Done by Person Bound by Law or Mistake of Fact Believing Himself Bound
Nothing is an offence done by person bound by law to do it, or by mistake of fact believing himself bound.

Section 77: Act of Judge When Acting Judicially
Nothing is an offence done by Judge when acting judicially in exercise of powers believed in good faith to be given by law.

Section 78: Act Done Pursuant to Judgment or Order of Court
Nothing is an offence done in pursuance of judgment or order of Court of Justice, if the person believes in good faith he is bound by law to do it.

Section 79: Act Done by Person Justified by Law
Nothing is an offence if done by any person justified by law or by mistake of fact believing himself justified by law.

Section 80: Accident in Doing a Lawful Act
Nothing is an offence done by accident or misfortune and without criminal intention or knowledge while doing lawful act in lawful manner with proper care and caution.

Section 81: Act Likely to Cause Harm but Done Without Criminal Intent and to Prevent Other Harm
Nothing is an offence merely by reason of harm caused if done without criminal intention in good faith to prevent other harm.

Section 82: Act of a Child Under Seven Years of Age
Nothing is an offence done by child under 7 years of age.

Section 83: Act of a Child Above Seven and Under Twelve of Immature Understanding
Nothing is an offence done by child above 7 and under 12 who has not attained sufficient maturity of understanding to judge nature and consequences.

Section 84: Act of a Person of Unsound Mind
Nothing is an offence done by person who is incapable of knowing nature of act or that it is wrong or contrary to law due to unsoundness of mind.

Section 85: Act of Person Incapable of Judgment by Intoxication Caused Against His Will
Nothing is an offence done by person intoxicated against his will without his knowledge.

Section 86: Offence Requiring Particular Intent or Knowledge Committed by One Who is Intoxicated
If person does act while intoxicated which would be offence if done with particular knowledge or intent, same knowledge/intent presumed unless intoxication was caused without his knowledge/will.

Section 87: Act Not Intended and Not Known to be Likely to Cause Death or Grievous Hurt, Done by Consent
Nothing is an offence if not intended to cause death/grievous hurt and not known likely to cause same, done by consent (express or implied).

Section 88: Act Not Intended to Cause Death, Done by Consent in Good Faith for Person's Benefit
Nothing is an offence if not intended to cause death, done by consent for person's benefit (e.g., medical procedures).

Section 89: Act Done in Good Faith for Benefit of Child or Insane Person, by Consent of Guardian
Act done in good faith for benefit of child under 12 or insane person by consent of guardian is not an offence.

Section 90: Consent Known to be Given Under Fear or Misconception
Consent not valid if given under fear of injury or misconception of fact, and offender knows or has reason to believe consent given under such circumstances.

Section 91: Exclusion of Acts Which are Offences Independently of Harm Caused
Exceptions in Sections 87-89 do not extend to acts which are offences independently (e.g., causing miscarriage without woman's consent).

Section 92: Act Done in Good Faith for Benefit of Person Without Consent
Act done in good faith for benefit of person without consent is not an offence if circumstances prevent consent or if person incapable of giving consent (e.g., emergency medical treatment).

Section 93: Communication Made in Good Faith
No communication made in good faith is an offence by reason of harm to person to whom it is made.

Section 94: Act to Which Person is Compelled by Threats
Except murder and offences against State punishable with death, nothing is an offence done by person compelled by threats to do it.

Section 95: Act Causing Slight Harm
Nothing is an offence by reason of slight harm where act done in good faith without criminal intent.

Section 96: Things Done in Private Defence
Nothing is an offence done in exercise of right of private defence.

Section 97: Right of Private Defence of Body and Property
Every person has right to defend own body and property of another, subject to restrictions in Section 99.

Section 98: Right of Private Defence Against Act of Person of Unsound Mind, etc.
Right exists even if act defended against is not an offence due to unsoundness of mind, intoxication, etc.

Section 99: Acts Against Which There is No Right of Private Defence
No right of private defence: against act not reasonably causing apprehension of death/grievous hurt; against public servant acting in good faith; more harm than necessary; when there is time to seek protection of public authorities.

Section 100: When Right of Private Defence of Body Extends to Causing Death
Right extends to causing death when reasonable apprehension of: death, grievous hurt, rape, unnatural offence, kidnapping/abduction, wrongful confinement with inability to seek public protection, acid attack.

Section 101: When Such Right Extends to Causing Any Harm Other Than Death
Right extends to causing harm (not death) when reasonable apprehension of assault not reasonably causing death/grievous hurt.

Section 102: Commencement and Continuance of Right of Private Defence of Body
Right commences when reasonable apprehension arises and continues as long as such apprehension continues.

Section 103: When Right of Private Defence of Property Extends to Causing Death
Right extends to causing death when: robbery, house-breaking by night, mischief by fire, theft/mischief/trespass where inability to seek public protection and reasonable apprehension of death/grievous hurt.

Section 104: When Such Right Extends to Causing Any Harm Other Than Death
Right extends to causing harm (not death) for: theft, mischief, criminal trespass.

Section 105: Commencement and Continuance of Right of Private Defence of Property
Right commences when reasonable apprehension of danger to property and continues while such danger continues.

Section 106: Right of Private Defence Against Deadly Assault When There is Risk of Harm to Innocent Person
If in exercise of right person kills innocent person, it is not an offence if there was no other way to defend and precautions taken.

=== CHAPTER V: ABETMENT ===

Section 107: Abetment of a Thing
Person abets if: instigates, engages in conspiracy, or intentionally aids commission of that thing.

Section 108: Abettor
Person who abets offence is called abettor.

Section 108A: Abetment in India of Offences Outside India
Person commits offence of abetment in India even if act abetted is done outside India.

Section 109: Punishment of Abetment if Act Abetted is Committed
Punished with punishment for offence abetted.

Section 110: Punishment of Abetment if Person Abetted Does Act with Different Intention
Abettor liable for act done with different intention if it was probable consequence.

Section 111: Liability of Abettor When One Act Abetted and Different Act Done
Abettor liable when different act is done if it was probable consequence of abetment.

Section 112: Abettor When Liable to Cumulative Punishment for Act Abetted and for Act Done
If act abetted and different act done are separate offences, abettor liable for both.

Section 113: Liability of Abettor for Effect Caused by Act Abetted Different from That Intended
Abettor liable for effects caused different from intended if act was probable consequence.

Section 114: Abettor Present When Offence is Committed
Whenever person abets offence and is present when committed, deemed to have committed offence.

Section 115: Abetment of Offence Punishable with Death or Imprisonment for Life
If offence not committed: imprisonment up to 7 years and fine.

Section 116: Abetment of Offence Punishable with Imprisonment
If offence not committed: imprisonment up to 1/4 of longest term or fine or both.

Section 117: Abetting Commission of Offence by Public or by More Than Ten Persons
Imprisonment up to 3 years or fine or both.

Section 118: Concealing Design to Commit Offence Punishable with Death or Imprisonment for Life
Imprisonment up to 7 years and fine.

Section 119: Public Servant Concealing Design to Commit Offence
Public servant: imprisonment up to 10 years and fine; others: imprisonment up to 3 years or fine or both.

Section 120: Concealing Design to Commit Offence Punishable with Imprisonment
Imprisonment up to 1/4 of longest term or fine or both.

Section 120A: Definition of Criminal Conspiracy
Agreement between two or more persons to do illegal act or legal act by illegal means.

Section 120B: Punishment of Criminal Conspiracy
If conspiracy to commit offence punishable with death/life/rigorous imprisonment for 2+ years: same as abetment; otherwise: imprisonment up to 6 months or fine or both.

=== CHAPTER VI: OFFENCES AGAINST THE STATE ===

Section 121: Waging or Attempting to Wage War Against Government of India
Punishment: Death or imprisonment for life and fine.

Section 121A: Conspiracy to Commit Offences Punishable by Section 121
Imprisonment for life or imprisonment up to 10 years and fine.

Imprisonment for life or imprisonment up to 10 years and fine.

Imprisonment up to 10 years and fine.

Imprisonment up to 7 years and fine.

Section 124A: Sedition
Whoever attempts to bring hatred/contempt/excite disaffection towards Government: imprisonment for life with/without fine or imprisonment up to 3 years with/without fine.

Section 125: Waging War Against Any Asiatic Power in Alliance with Government of India
Imprisonment for life and fine, or imprisonment up to 7 years and fine, or fine.

Section 126: Committing Depredation on Territories of Power at Peace with Government of India
Imprisonment up to 7 years and fine, and forfeiture of property seized.

Section 127: Receiving Property Taken by War or Depredation
Imprisonment up to 7 years and fine.

Section 128: Public Servant Voluntarily Allowing Prisoner to Escape
Imprisonment up to 3 years or fine or both; if prisoner under sentence of death: imprisonment for life or imprisonment up to 10 years and fine.

Section 129: Public Servant Negligently Suffering Prisoner to Escape
Simple imprisonment up to 2 years or fine or both.

Section 130: Aiding Escape of, Rescuing or Harboring Prisoner
Imprisonment up to 7 years and fine (varies based on sentence prisoner was under).

Imprisonment up to 6 months or fine or both; if hired for armed unlawful assembly: imprisonment up to 2 years or fine or both.

When two or more persons fight in public place disturbing public peace.

Imprisonment up to 1 month or fine up to Rs. 100 or both.

Causing death by doing act with intention or knowledge of likely causing death.

Culpable homicide is murder if act done with specific intentions/knowledge (with exceptions for provocation, private defence, etc.).
